We find ourselves immersed in a time marked by two contradictory forces, interdependence and diversity. The tensions from these twin forces are rapidly rendering traditional leadership behaviors obsolete (Lipman-Blumen, 1996, p. 3). Instead, we must broaden our leadership repertoire of related behaviors to maximize the use of self and others as instruments for accomplishing goals. The Connective Leadership Model outlined below lists 3 sets of achieving styles that define a connective leader; direct, instrumental, and relational. Beneath this model, you will find composites graphs illustrating the results of completed Individual and 360 degree Achieving Styles Inventories (ASI).
